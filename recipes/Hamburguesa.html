Tailor Made Burgers

about 10 large or 20 mini hamburgers come out, like the ones I made

  • 1kg minced meat
  • 1 raw chorizo (criollo chorizo, if you are in Spain, or another that seems to you)
  • 2 green onions (or 1 common onion)
  • 2 eggs (optional)
  • 2 cloves of garlic
  • 1 tsp paprika
  • 1 tbsp. chopped parsley
  • Salt (about 2 tsp) and pepper
  1. It's very easy! The first thing is to mix the minced meat well with the chorizo, remove the inside of the chorizo ​​and discard the skin.
  2. Once this is done, add the finely chopped onions and garlic, salt, pepper, paprika and parsley, also chopped.
  3. Once this is integrated, add the two eggs and mix well with your hands. You can add breadcrumbs to it (half a cup, no more). But these proportions work very well for me and the meat is just the way I want it. The egg is optional. Hamburgers are usually egg-free. I like how it is.
  4. Make homemade burgers. They can be made with mold. I like them made by hand, so that you can see that they are homemade. This time I made mini burgers.
  5. To cook: do I have to explain to you how to make a homemade hamburger? Yes? Good: heat a griddle or frying pan (very hot), put the hamburger and when it is on one side, turn it over (turn it over once). Until it's from the other. I accompanied them this time with a yamani rice with vegetables. This is because the hamburger is very discredited and a "healthy" side dish forces us to rethink (and reassess) its place on the plate.
